Mapping seagrass in the Mediterranean

1 - Project Overview

Mitigating the loss of seagrass meadows is a growing concern as it still is occurring uncontrollably in the Mediterranean. Seagrass meadows are vital for both humans and wildlife. Countering climate change, supporting the fishing-yields necessary for tourists’ ocean dinners, providing carbon storage needed for moderation of shoreline carbonic acid levels, and maintaining the “Circle of Life” needed by marine life are only a few essentials seagrass meadow populations provide. In Europe alone, over a third of the seagrass meadows were lost from 1869 to 2016 (de los Santos et al.). Time and effort is actively being directed towards seagrass meadow restoration as we speak, but lacks effectiveness when restoration-workers do not know where to prioritize their efforts. The goal of this project is to aid the monitoring and restoration of seagrass meadow depletion through the application of Computer Vision on satellite imagery.
